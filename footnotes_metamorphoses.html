footnotes: metamorphoses

by Matthew Stokdyk

Q: What’s going to happen to her now?
A: She’s going to suffer.
Q: And?
A: She’s going to suffer.
Q: And?
A: She’s going to suffer.

From the “Eros and Psyche” episode of Mary Zimmerman’s Metamorphoses.†

† Of note, the tale of Cupid and Psyche isn’t actually found in Ovid’s Metamorphoses; Zimmerman has introduced it from another source.1

1 a footnote is unimportant;
 it demands no attention.
 nevertheless, it persists,
 edifying, nagging, citing
 and with snark.

 it is subjugated, not the story,
 verse from verse
 born from reaction;a
 but it’s not truly important,
 not worthy of the body.b

feeble words, notes set below— subaltern words, my low language like Dante in Paradisec pained by the meagerness of human tongues. the old poets prayed for high language to be lifted up up upd out of the dark; but it’s the modern impulse to beg to be lowered, shoved into the mire and taste the mud. so that’s what I do, footnotes sinking down down down, because undeniably the mud has a rich vintage. it was for a paper on Pompeiie that I was made to use Chicago style; MLA, my loving mother, frowns upon footnotes while the windy ones demand them. and ever since I’ve been a sucker; note after note after note with all the details, my post-modern sense and barren modern decadence to mark down my drivel. it’s all symbolic, really, evidence of disorder ingrained habituated even into verse, to write down my wrongs and then specify just what I did.f however, footnotes present a problem for readers: do you roll on, not stopping or pausing at each marker to see what secret might be divined? looking down to footnotes with each breath tends to fragment the whole poemg to tear it to bits and render it absolutely incoherent.h but perhaps that’s best, most accurate to life to glimpse the parentheticalnessi of hands so compelled to scribble down everything. _______________ a alluding to Eliot here would suffice, but I don’t know if my mind is platinum; platinum doesn’t rust. b as I a footnote to yours, unworthy c Oh quanto è corto il dire e come fioco al mio concetto! d what in me is dark Illumine, what is low raise and support, or μῆνιν ἄειδε θεὰ (but whose wrath? mine, or the reader’s? or even Achilles?)I e I would bury all of Manhattan, too, if I could learn to write a decent villanelle. f I believe in every one writing poetry who wants to; most do. I believe in every man knowing enough of music to play “God bless our home” on the harmonium, but I do not believe in every man giving concerts and printing his sin.II g to prove a point I must put this here; but pretend I meant to quote These fragments I have shored against my ruinsIII h If Napoleon if I told him if I told him if Napoleon.
